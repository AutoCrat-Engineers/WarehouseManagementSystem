WMS UI - Comprehensive Sidebar Icon Animations Log
================================================

This document outlines the custom CSS animations engineered for the entire sidebar navigation menu. By utilizing SVG path targeting `svg path:nth-child()`, we break apart static Lucide React icons and animate their individual components on hover.

GLOBAL HOVER STYLING
-----------------------
* PREVIOUS: SVGs utilized generic color fading and a global drop-shadow glow filter.
* PRESENT: Drop-shadow filters removed to prevent blurry sub-pixel rendering. Hardware acceleration (`translateZ(0)`, `backface-visibility: hidden`) applied globally/per-icon to ensure perfectly crisp vector edges during continuous rotation or movement. Only stroke colors change alongside the structural animations.

CORE MODULE ICONS
-----------------------
1. DASHBOARD (LayoutDashboard)
   - Animation: The four squares detach. Top-left and bottom-right slide inwards, while top-right and bottom-left slide outwards, creating a diagonal positional swap before snapping backward.

2. ITEM MASTER (Package)
   - Animation: The tape line (4th path) is permanently hidden to create a clean open box. On hover, the entire box executes a smooth, continuous 360-degree counter-clockwise rotation, fully stabilized by CSS GPU compositing.

3. INVENTORY (Boxes)
   - Animation: The bottom two foundational boxes slide slightly outward (left and right) to make room, while the top box dynamically drops into place from above.

4. STOCK MOVEMENTS (ArrowRightLeft)
   - Animation: The top right-facing arrow and bottom left-facing arrow visibly detach and translate across each other, physically exchanging positions in an infinite loop.

5. PACKING (PackageOpen)
   - Animation: Uses orthographic 3D mathematics (`rotateX(60deg)` pinned to the `center bottom`). This pulls the angled isometric flaps flush downward towards the base, perfectly simulating smooth opening and closing movements without breaking or detaching the vector paths.

6. RACK VIEW (Grid3x3)
   - Animation: Simulates a laser warehouse scanner. The outer perimeter pulses, followed by the internal horizontal and vertical grid lines illuminating and drawing themselves one by one in a rapid sequential sequence.

7. BLANKET ORDERS (FileText)
   - Animation: The top-right folded corner physically peels backward. Moments later, the internal text lines "typewrite" into existence, sliding in from the left sequentially.

8. BLANKET RELEASES (Calendar)
   - Animation: The top binding pins bounce upward, and the entire calendar body rotates deeply on its X-axis to simulate a page turning over.

9. FORECASTING (TrendingUp)
   - Animation: The main trend polyline animates its `stroke-dasharray` to literally draw itself from bottom-left to top-right. Seconds later, the arrowhead pops into existence at the peak.

10. USER MANAGEMENT (Users)
    - Animation: A subtle, unified rocking/wiggling motion applied to the entire people cluster, adding character without breaking the complex inner paths.

11. DISPATCH (CargoShip)
    - Animation: Stripped of overlapping semi-transparent layers to fix historical "shadiw/blurring" issues. The ship now cleanly floats/bobs on a pure hardware-accelerated Z-axis, while a single, fully opaque (crisp) water line rolls endlessly underneath.


SUB-MODULE & WORKFLOW ICONS
-----------------------
12. STICKER GENERATION (Printer)
    - Animation: The internal paper tray feeds downward, and the bottom printed page physically slides out of the printer housing.

13. PACKING DETAILS & MPL HOME (ClipboardList)
    - Animation: The checklist items execute a rapid, staggered left-to-right slide, simulating tasks being "ticked off" one after another.

14. PALLET DASHBOARD (Layers)
    - Animation: The three stacked isometric layers fan apart vertically to reveal the spacing between them, then smoothly collapse back into a tightly bound stack.

15. DISPATCH SELECTION (Forklift)
    - Animation: The two circular tires spin continuously utilizing `rotate(360deg)`, while the body of the forklift rocks forward and backward to simulate driving.

16. PROFORMA INVOICE (Stamp)
    - Animation: The handle and inkpad lift upward, delay, and then slam firmly downward onto the surface path. Upon impact, the base line flashes green and pulses, visually confirming an "Approved" stamp.
